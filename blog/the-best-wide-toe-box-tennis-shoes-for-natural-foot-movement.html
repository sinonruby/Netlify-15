Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a "zero-drop" shoe, and is it good for tennis?

A zero-drop shoe means the heel and forefoot are at the same height from the ground, promoting a more natural, flat-footed stance. It can be excellent for tennis as it strengthens foot muscles and improves balance, but itâ€™s crucial to transition slowly to avoid injury.

Can I use running shoes with a wide toe box for tennis?

While brands like WHITIN and BRONAX market their shoes for "tennis," dedicated tennis shoes offer crucial lateral support and durable outsoles designed for the specific demands of the court. Running shoes are made for forward motion and may not provide the stability needed for side-to-side cuts, increasing injury risk.

Do I need to size up in wide toe box shoes?

Not necessarily. A well-designed wide toe box shoe should fit your foot length correctly while providing extra width room. However, some models like the Babolat SFX4 recommend going down half a size, so always check specific brand guidelines and reviews.

Are these shoes only for people with "wide feet"?

No! Anyone can benefit from a wider toe box. Even if you don't have a wide foot, allowing your toes to splay naturally can improve your balance, stability, and overall comfort on the court.
